I am a Ph.D. student (dissertation submitted) at the [Department of Electrical Communication Engineering](https://ece.iisc.ac.in/) at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru. I am advised by [Prof. Navin Kashyap](https://ece.iisc.ac.in/~nkashyap/). I received the B.E. (Hons.) degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from BITS, Pilani--Hyderabad Campus, in 2018, graduating with a gold medal. 

My research interests are broadly in the field of information theory, with particular focus on the computation of capacity of finite-state channels. A part of my work towards my Ph.D. thesis was awarded the [IEEE Jack Keil Wolf ISIT Student Paper Award](https://www.itsoc.org/honors/wolf-award#:~:text=The%20IEEE%20Jack%20Keil%20Wolf,the%20principal%20author%20and%20presenter.) at the ISIT 2023. I have, in the past, worked on the design of codes for distributed storage, and on matchings in bipartite graphs, with preferences. 

My research at IISc was generously supported by [Qualcomm Innovation Fellowships (QIF) India](https://www.qualcomm.com/research/university-relations/innovation-fellowship) 2020 and 2022 and by a Prime Minister's Research Fellowship (PMRF) 2020.
